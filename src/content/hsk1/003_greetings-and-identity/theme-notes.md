# Week 3 — Greetings, Pronouns & Identity

This week you can hold your first real conversation. The pieces are: pronouns, a verb (是 *shì*, "to be"), and one tiny particle (的) that does the work of "of / my / 's" / adjective marker.

## The 是 sentence (noun-only)

The 是 sentence equates two noun phrases. The pattern is rigid:

> **Subject + 是 + Noun-Predicate**

| Sentence | Pinyin | English |
|----------|--------|---------|
| 我是学生。 | Wǒ shì xuéshēng. | I am a student. |
| 他是老师。 | Tā shì lǎoshī. | He is a teacher. |
| 这是我的朋友。 | Zhè shì wǒ de péngyou. | This is my friend. |
| 我不是中国人。 | Wǒ bú shì Zhōngguó rén. | I am not Chinese. |

**Critical rule (sets up next week's error-killer):** 是 is **only** for noun = noun statements. With *adjectives*, you do **not** use 是 — you use 很 instead. Wrong: ✘ 我是好. Right (week 4): ✓ 我很好. Pre-empt this trap now.

Negation: 不是 (`bú shì` — note the tone-4 sandhi from week 1).

## The 的 particle — two jobs you'll see this week

### Job 1: possessive

> **Owner + 的 + Thing**

| Sentence | English |
|----------|---------|
| 我的名字 | my name |
| 你的老师 | your teacher |
| 他的朋友 | his friend |

For close personal relationships (family members, especially), 的 is often **dropped**: 我妈妈 instead of 我的妈妈. Both are correct; the bare form is more colloquial and warmer.

### Job 2: modifier marker (preview — you'll use it more in weeks 4–7)

Anything before 的 modifies the noun after it. The "of" feeling is the same:

- 中国的朋友 → "a friend from China" / "Chinese friend"

## Pronouns at a glance

| Singular | Plural |
|----------|--------|
| 我 wǒ — I, me | 我们 wǒmen — we, us |
| 你 nǐ — you | 你们 nǐmen — you (pl.) |
| 他 tā — he, him | 他们 tāmen — they (masc. / mixed) |
| 她 tā — she, her | 她们 tāmen — they (feminine only) |
| 它 tā — it (rare in HSK 1) | — |

In writing, 他/她/它 are distinct. In speech they are identical — context disambiguates.

## Conversation pack

| Function | Phrase |
|----------|--------|
| Hello | 你好 (nǐ hǎo) |
| Hello (polite, to elders / strangers) | 您好 (nín hǎo) |
| Thank you | 谢谢 (xièxie) |
| You're welcome | 不客气 (bú kèqi) |
| Sorry | 对不起 (duìbuqǐ) |
| It's okay | 没关系 (méi guānxi) |
| Goodbye | 再见 (zàijiàn) — literally "again see" |
| What's your name? | 你叫什么名字？ (nǐ jiào shénme míngzi?) |
| My name is X. | 我叫 X. (wǒ jiào X.) |

Notice 叫 (jiào, "to be called") is the common form for names — more natural than 是. Both are accepted in HSK 1: 我叫小明 / 我的名字是小明.
