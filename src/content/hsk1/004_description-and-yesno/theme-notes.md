# Week 4 — Description & Yes/No Questions

This week's structural lesson is short and ruthless: **adjectives in Chinese are not "be + adjective". They are stand-alone predicates.** Once that flips in your head, half the beginner errors vanish.

## Adjectives use 很, not 是

Wrong (by literal English-to-Chinese translation):
> ✘ 我是高。

Right:
> ✓ 我很高。 (Wǒ hěn gāo.) — *I am tall.*

The verb "to be" disappears entirely. 高 itself is the predicate. 很 fills the slot in front of it.

### What is 很 doing?

In isolation, 很 means "very", but **most of the time it has no meaning at all** — it's a structural placeholder. A bare adjective without 很 reads as a **comparison** rather than a statement:

| Sentence | Reading |
|----------|---------|
| 我高。 | "I'm taller (than someone)." (Implicit comparison.) |
| 我很高。 | "I am tall." (Neutral statement.) |
| 我真高。 | "I am really tall." (Stronger.) |
| 我非常高。 | "I am extremely tall." |

If you genuinely mean "very," many native speakers will stress it or swap in 真 / 非常 / 太. Bare 很 is the unmarked default.

## Negation: 不

Adjective predicates negate with 不 (not 没):

| Sentence | English |
|----------|---------|
| 我不高。 | I'm not tall. |
| 这个不好。 | This one isn't good. |
| 她不冷。 | She's not cold. |

## Yes/no questions with 吗

Tack 吗 (`ma`, neutral tone) onto the end of a statement to turn it into a yes/no question. Word order does not change.

| Statement | Question |
|-----------|----------|
| 你是学生。 | 你是学生吗？ |
| 他很高。 | 他高吗？ (the 很 drops in questions and short answers) |
| 这是中国茶。 | 这是中国茶吗？ |

### Answering

There is no single word for "yes" or "no". You **echo the verb or adjective**.

| Question | Affirmative | Negative |
|----------|-------------|----------|
| 你是学生吗？ | 是。 / 我是。 | 不是。 |
| 你冷吗？ | 冷。 / 我很冷。 | 不冷。 |
| 你有书吗？ | 有。 | 没有。 (preview of week 5) |

This is why "adjective uses 很" matters so much: it changes how you answer questions about yourself in every conversation.
