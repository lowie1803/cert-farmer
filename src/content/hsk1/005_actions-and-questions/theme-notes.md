# Week 5 — Actions & Question Words

You can now describe people and things. This week you make them **do** things, and you learn how to ask information questions.

## S-V-O — like English, but stricter

Basic word order is **Subject + Verb + Object**, identical to English on the surface:

| Sentence | English |
|----------|---------|
| 我吃饭。 | I eat (a meal). |
| 他喝水。 | He drinks water. |
| 她说中文。 | She speaks Chinese. |
| 我们看书。 | We read books. |

Two rules that differ from English:

1. **No subject-verb agreement**, no conjugation. 我吃 / 你吃 / 他吃 / 我们吃 — verb form never changes.
2. **No bare future or past form** — those need particles (week 7 introduces 了; "tomorrow I'll go" uses time words, not a verb change).

## 不 vs 没有 — the single most-tested HSK-1 contrast

There are **two** ways to negate a verb. They are not interchangeable.

| Particle | What it negates | Tense feel |
|----------|------------------|-----------|
| 不 (bù) | Habits, intentions, present/future state. | "doesn't / won't" |
| 没(有) (méi yǒu) | Possession (有) and completed actions (preview of week 7). | "doesn't have / didn't" |

> 🚫 **不有 is never correct.** 有 takes 没 as its negation, period.

| Right | Wrong | English |
|-------|-------|---------|
| 我没有钱。 | ✘ 我不有钱。 | I don't have money. |
| 我不想吃。 | — | I don't want to eat. |
| 我不喝茶。 | — | I don't drink tea. (habit) |

Rule of thumb: **不 = won't / don't (by choice or habit). 没 = lacking / didn't happen.**

## Question words — they don't move

In English, question words jump to the front: *"What do you eat?"* In Chinese, the question word **stays where the answer would sit**.

| Statement (with placeholder) | Question | English |
|------------------------------|----------|---------|
| 我吃饭。 | 你吃什么？ | What do you eat? |
| 他是学生。 | 他是谁？ | Who is he? |
| 她在家。 | 她在哪儿？ | Where is she? |
| 我有三本书。 | 你有几本书？ | How many books do you have? |

The five HSK-1 question words:

| Word | Pinyin | Asks about | Slot |
|------|--------|------------|------|
| 什么 | shénme | what | object / noun |
| 谁 | shéi / shuí | who | subject / object |
| 哪儿 / 哪里 | nǎr / nǎlǐ | where | location |
| 几 | jǐ | how many (small numbers, < 10, expects a number answer) | quantifier slot |
| 多少 | duōshao | how many / how much (any quantity, esp. > 10 or price) | quantifier slot |

> 💡 **吗 vs question words:** Never combine them. ✘ 你吃什么吗？ is wrong. 吗 is for yes/no; question words already mark the sentence as a question.
