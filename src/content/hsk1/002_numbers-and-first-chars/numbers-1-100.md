# Numbers 一 to 百

Chinese numbers are spectacularly regular up to 99. There are only **eleven** atoms: 一 二 三 四 五 六 七 八 九 十 百.

## The atoms

| Hanzi | Pinyin | Number |
|-------|--------|--------|
| 零 / 〇 | líng | 0 |
| 一 | yī | 1 |
| 二 | èr | 2 |
| 三 | sān | 3 |
| 四 | sì | 4 |
| 五 | wǔ | 5 |
| 六 | liù | 6 |
| 七 | qī | 7 |
| 八 | bā | 8 |
| 九 | jiǔ | 9 |
| 十 | shí | 10 |
| 百 | bǎi | 100 |

## Composition rules (11–99)

- 11 = 十一 (literally "ten one"). 12 = 十二. … 19 = 十九.
- 20 = 二十 ("two ten"). 30 = 三十. … 90 = 九十.
- 21 = 二十一. 35 = 三十五. 99 = 九十九.

That's it. Read the digits left-to-right; the ten-position is "n 十", and you tack on the ones if any.

## Hundreds (100–999)

- 100 = 一百. (You **must** say *yī* before 百, unlike 10 where 十 stands alone.)
- 200 = 二百 **or** more commonly 两百 (see below).
- 101 = 一百零一. **A zero is spoken** when there's a gap in the middle. 105 = 一百零五.
- 110 = 一百一十. The 十 is required.
- 250 = 二百五十 / 两百五十.

## 两 vs 二 — the single trap

Both mean "two", but they are not interchangeable:

| Use 二 | Use 两 |
|--------|--------|
| In numbers and counting: 二十, 一百二十 | Before a measure word: 两个人, 两本书 |
| Ordinals: 第二 (second) | "Two of something concrete": 两年, 两点 (2 o'clock) |
| In mathematical expressions and phone numbers | When the next word is a measure word or quasi-MW like 年/天/点 |

Mnemonic: **二 is the number**, **两 is the quantity**. "The number two" → 二. "Two **of** something" → 两.

## 一 tone shifts (sandhi review)

You met this in week 1, but here it bites hard:

- 一 yī by itself or in number sequences (一百, 一二三): **tone 1**.
- 一 before a tone-4 syllable: **tone 2**. 一个 (yí ge), 一岁 (yí suì).
- 一 before tones 1, 2, or 3: **tone 4**. 一年 (yì nián), 一天 (yì tiān), 一本 (yì běn).

Don't try to memorize the rule mid-sentence. Drill the common collocations until they feel automatic.
