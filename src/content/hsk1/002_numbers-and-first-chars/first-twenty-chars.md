# First 20 High-Frequency Characters

These twenty characters re-use last week's pictographic components and unlock 70%+ of HSK 1 sentences. Notice how often 人, 口, and 日 appear inside.

| Character | Pinyin | Meaning | Component breakdown |
|-----------|--------|---------|---------------------|
| 你 | nǐ | you | 亻(person) + 尔 |
| 我 | wǒ | I, me | hand (扌-like) + 戈 (a halberd) — historically "self with weapon" |
| 他 | tā | he | 亻(person) + 也 |
| 她 | tā | she | 女(woman) + 也 |
| 们 | men | plural marker | 亻(person) + 门 (door, phonetic *mén*) |
| 是 | shì | to be (am/is/are) | 日(sun) on top + 正 (correct) bottom |
| 不 | bù | not | originally a flower with downward sepals — a "no" gesture |
| 的 | de | possessive / modifier particle | 白(white) + 勺 (ladle) |
| 好 | hǎo | good | 女(woman) + 子(child) — "woman with child" |
| 大 | dà | big | a person 人 with arms outstretched |
| 小 | xiǎo | small | three dots, like grains of sand |
| 中 | zhōng | middle, China | a vertical line through a box (a spear through a target's middle) |
| 国 | guó | country | 囗(enclosure) + 玉(jade) — "treasure inside borders" |

## What to do with this list

You do **not** have to be able to *write* every one this week. The targets, in order:

1. **Recognize** all 20 at a glance, with correct pinyin and tone.
2. **Decompose** each into its components on demand. "What's inside 好?" → "女 + 子, woman + child."
3. **Spot** them inside larger characters you'll meet later. (国 will reappear in 中国, 美国, 国家.)
