# Week 6 — Time, Dates & Measure Words

## Measure words — the tax you pay on every noun

You cannot say "three books" in Chinese the way English does. You **must** insert a measure word (classifier) between the number and the noun:

> **Number + Measure Word + Noun**

| Wrong | Right | English |
|-------|-------|---------|
| ✘ 三书 | ✓ 三**本**书 | three books |
| ✘ 一人 | ✓ 一**个**人 | one person |
| ✘ 五茶 | ✓ 五**杯**茶 | five (cups of) tea |

Every concrete noun has a "correct" measure word. The good news: **个 (gè)** is the universal default — when you don't know the specific one, 个 is almost always understood. The bad news: native speech sounds rough if you default to 个 for everything.

### HSK 1 measure word starter pack

| MW | Pinyin | Used for | Examples |
|----|--------|----------|----------|
| 个 | gè | general default, people | 一个人, 三个朋友, 几个学生 |
| 本 | běn | bound items (books) | 一本书 |
| 杯 | bēi | cups / glasses | 一杯茶, 两杯水 |
| 只 | zhī | small animals | 一只狗 |
| 张 | zhāng | flat items (paper, tickets, tables) | 三张票 |
| 件 | jiàn | items of clothing, matters | 两件衣服 |
| 口 | kǒu | members of a household | 我家有四口人 |
| 块 | kuài | pieces; also colloquial currency | 五块钱 |

### 两 vs 二 (review week 2 — applied here)

When a measure word follows, **use 两, not 二**.

| Wrong | Right |
|-------|-------|
| ✘ 二本书 | ✓ 两本书 |
| ✘ 二个人 | ✓ 两个人 |
| ✘ 二杯茶 | ✓ 两杯茶 |

二 is still correct in counting (二十, 第二, 一二三). The rule is one-liner: **before a measure word, 两**.

## Dates and time — big to small

Chinese dates are stated **year → month → day → weekday → time**, the opposite of American conventional order.

### Year

> Year is read **digit by digit**, then 年. 2026 = 二〇二六年 (èr líng èr liù nián). Not 'two thousand twenty-six'.

### Month and day

| Hanzi | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| 一月 / 二月 ... 十二月 | January / February ... December |
| 三号 / 三日 | the 3rd (号 in speech, 日 in writing) |

### Weekday

> 星期 (xīngqī) + number 1–6 for Monday–Saturday. Sunday = **星期天** or **星期日** (no number).

| Hanzi | Day |
|-------|-----|
| 星期一 | Monday |
| 星期二 | Tuesday |
| 星期三 | Wednesday |
| 星期四 | Thursday |
| 星期五 | Friday |
| 星期六 | Saturday |
| 星期天 / 星期日 | Sunday |

### Time of day

> Hour = number + 点 (diǎn). Minutes = number + 分.
> *2 o'clock* = **两**点 (not 二点 — measure-word-like behavior).

| Phrase | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| 现在几点？ | What time is it now? |
| 三点 | 3 o'clock |
| 三点半 | 3:30 (literally "3 o'clock half") |
| 三点十五分 | 3:15 |
| 差五分三点 | 5 minutes to 3 |

### Today / tomorrow / yesterday

| Hanzi | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| 今天 jīntiān | today |
| 明天 míngtiān | tomorrow |
| 昨天 zuótiān | yesterday |
| 现在 xiànzài | now |

These are **nouns**, not adverbs — they sit before the verb or at the start of the sentence: 我今天吃饭 / 今天我吃饭. Both work.
