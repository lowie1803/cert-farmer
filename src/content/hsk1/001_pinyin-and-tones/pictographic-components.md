# Eight Pictographic Components

These eight characters are not vocabulary — yet. You are meeting them as **components**: building blocks that show up inside hundreds of other characters. Recognizing the picture inside a character is the single highest-leverage skill a self-learner can install in week 1.

| Component | Pinyin | Meaning | Pictograph it came from | Appears inside |
|-----------|--------|---------|--------------------------|----------------|
| 人 | rén | person | A side-view of a walking person | 们, 你, 他, 从, 众, 休 |
| 口 | kǒu | mouth | A square open mouth | 吃, 喝, 说, 叫, 名, 知 |
| 日 | rì | sun / day | A circle with a dot (the sun) | 时, 早, 明, 晚, 春, 是 |
| 月 | yuè | moon / month | A crescent moon | 朋, 期, 朝, 望; also "flesh/body" inside 肉/脸/胖 |
| 木 | mù | tree / wood | A tree with branches and roots | 林, 森, 校, 树, 本, 来 |
| 水 | shuǐ | water | Three flowing streams (full form) / 氵 (radical form on the left) | 河, 湖, 海, 江, 没, 法 |
| 山 | shān | mountain | Three peaks | 岛, 岩, 峰, 出 |
| 火 | huǒ | fire | A flame with sparks (full form) / 灬 at the bottom | 灯, 烧, 热, 然, 照 |

## How to study these *this week*

1. **Trace once, daily.** Open any stroke-order app, write each of the eight characters in the air or on paper. You are not memorizing — you are letting the shape settle into your hand.
2. **Spot them in the wild.** Open any HSK 1 word list. Every time you see one of these components inside a more complex character, say its meaning out loud. 你 → "person 人 + you" sticks far better than rote.
3. **Notice the radical forms.** 水 becomes 氵 (three drops) on the left of a character. 火 becomes 灬 (four dots) at the bottom. 心 (heart) — which you'll meet next week — becomes 忄 on the left. Mandarin has a small set of these positional variants; learning them now means you will never wonder *"what does 氵 mean?"* in week 5.
