# Week 1 — Ongoing Action & Change of State

HSK 1 gave you static sentences ("I am a student", "I have three books") and one aspect particle (了 for completion). HSK 2 brings sentences to life. This week: the two structural pieces of *time-as-movement* — **ongoing** action (在 / 正在) and **change of state** (sentence-final 了).

## 在 — ongoing action

Stick **在 (zài)** before a verb and the action is *happening right now*:

> **Subject + 在 + Verb + (Object)**

| Sentence | English |
|----------|---------|
| 我在吃饭。 | I'm (in the middle of) eating. |
| 他在看书。 | He's reading (right now). |
| 你在做什么？ | What are you doing? |

You can add 正 in front of 在 for "right at this moment":

> 我**正在**吃饭呢。 — *I'm just in the middle of eating.*

The 呢 (`ne`) at the end is a softening particle — friendly, very common with 正在.

> ⚠️ **Don't confuse this 在 with location-在.** Location 在 needs a place after it: 我在家 (*I'm at home*). Ongoing 在 needs a **verb** after it: 我在吃 (*I'm eating*). Same character, two jobs.

## 了 — change of state

You already know **了** for completed actions (我吃了饭, week 7 of HSK 1). The same 了, when it sits at the end of a sentence, can also mark a **change of state** — something that wasn't true before is true now:

| Sentence | English | What changed |
|----------|---------|--------------|
| 我饿了。 | I'm hungry (now). | Was not hungry → am hungry. |
| 下雨了。 | It's started raining. | Was not raining → is raining. |
| 我会说中文了。 | I can speak Chinese now. | Couldn't → can. |
| 他十八岁了。 | He's eighteen (now). | Birthday just passed. |

The cue is **state-changed**, not "in the past". The sentence stays in present time; 了 reports a transition into that state.

### V-了-O (completion) vs S-Adj-了 (change of state)

| Pattern | Marks | Example |
|---------|-------|---------|
| **Subject + V + 了 + Object** | Completed action | 我吃了饭。 (I ate.) |
| **Subject + Adjective + 了** | Change of state | 我饿了。 (I've gotten hungry.) |
| **Subject + Verb + Object + 了** | Often: change of state or "now happening" | 下雨了。 (It's started raining.) |

If both apply, both 了 can show up in one sentence — but HSK 2 doesn't push that. For now, learn to recognize the two patterns.

## Frequency & ongoing adverbs

This week's adverb pack — all sit **before the verb**, like 也 and 都:

| Adverb | Pinyin | Meaning |
|--------|--------|---------|
| 常常 | chángcháng | often |
| 已经 | yǐjīng | already (usually paired with sentence-final 了) |
| 正在 | zhèngzài | right now (intensified 在) |
| 还 | hái | still / also |
| 都 | dōu | all / both (review from HSK 1) |

| Sentence | English |
|----------|---------|
| 我常常喝茶。 | I often drink tea. |
| 他已经吃饭了。 | He has already eaten. |
| 她正在睡觉。 | She's sleeping right now. |
| 我还想喝水。 | I still want to drink water. |

## Common verbs introduced this week

| Hanzi | Pinyin | Meaning |
|-------|--------|---------|
| 睡觉 | shuìjiào | to sleep |
| 工作 | gōngzuò | to work / a job |
| 学习 | xuéxí | to study |
| 看电视 | kàn diànshì | to watch TV |
| 打电话 | dǎ diànhuà | to make a phone call |

Note that several of these are **verb-object compounds**: 睡觉 = "sleep a sleep", 打电话 = "hit a phone-call". The object is usually fixed; you don't need to think of them as two words in HSK 2.
