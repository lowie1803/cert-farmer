# Mutual Funds

## What is a Mutual Fund?

A **mutual fund** pools money from many investors to buy a diversified portfolio of stocks, bonds, or other assets. A professional fund manager decides what to buy and sell.

Think of it as hiring someone to invest for you alongside thousands of other people.

## How They Work

1. You invest money into the fund
2. The fund manager buys securities with the pooled money
3. You own **shares of the fund**, not the individual stocks
4. The fund's **NAV (Net Asset Value)** is calculated once per day at market close
5. You can buy/sell shares at the end-of-day NAV

## Types of Mutual Funds

| Type | Strategy | Example |
|------|----------|---------|
| **Index funds** | Track an index passively | Vanguard 500 Index (VFIAX) |
| **Actively managed** | Manager picks stocks | Fidelity Contrafund (FCNTX) |
| **Bond funds** | Invest in bonds | PIMCO Total Return |
| **Balanced/Target-date** | Mix of stocks + bonds | Vanguard Target 2050 |
| **Sector funds** | Focus on one industry | Fidelity Select Technology |
| **Money market** | Very short-term, safe debt | Vanguard Federal MMF |

## Key Terms

| Term | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **NAV** | Net Asset Value — the fund's per-share price, calculated daily |
| **Expense ratio** | Annual fee as % of assets (e.g., 0.50% = $50/year per $10,000) |
| **Load** | Sales commission — front-end (buy), back-end (sell), or no-load |
| **Minimum investment** | Amount needed to open ($1,000-$3,000 typical) |
| **Turnover ratio** | How often the fund trades — high turnover = more taxes |

## Active vs Passive

| Feature | Active (Managed) | Passive (Index) |
|---------|-----------------|----------------|
| Goal | Beat the market | Match the market |
| Expense ratio | 0.50-1.50% | 0.03-0.20% |
| Manager | Makes decisions | Follows an index |
| Performance | Usually underperforms | Matches the index |
| Tax efficiency | Lower | Higher |

**The data is clear**: Over long periods, passive index funds beat most active managers, primarily because of lower fees.

## When Mutual Funds Make Sense

- **Retirement accounts** (401k, IRA) — often the only option available
- **Target-date funds** — great "set and forget" option for retirement
- **Automatic investing** — many allow recurring purchases with no minimum per transaction

## Red Flags

- Expense ratio above 1%
- Front-end or back-end loads (sales commissions)
- High turnover ratio (tax-inefficient)
- Consistently underperforming their benchmark

## Key Takeaway

> Mutual funds offer diversification and professional management. For most investors, low-cost index mutual funds or their ETF equivalents are the best choice. Avoid high fees and sales loads.
