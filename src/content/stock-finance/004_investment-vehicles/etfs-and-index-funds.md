# ETFs & Index Funds

## The Problem with Picking Individual Stocks

Picking winning stocks is extremely hard. Even professional fund managers fail to beat the market most of the time — over a 15-year period, **over 90%** of actively managed funds underperform the S&P 500.

This is why **index investing** has become the most popular strategy for regular investors.

## What is an Index Fund?

An index fund is a fund that **tracks a market index** by holding all (or most) of the stocks in that index.

Instead of picking individual winners, you buy the whole market.

```
S&P 500 Index Fund → holds ~500 stocks
Total Market Fund   → holds ~4,000 stocks
```

## What is an ETF?

An **Exchange-Traded Fund (ETF)** is a fund that trades on an exchange like a stock. Most popular ETFs are index funds.

| Feature | ETF | Mutual Fund (Index) |
|---------|-----|-------------------|
| Trades like | A stock (real-time) | End of day only |
| Minimum investment | Price of 1 share | Often $1-$3,000 |
| Expense ratio | Very low (0.03-0.20%) | Low (0.03-0.50%) |
| Tax efficiency | More efficient | Less efficient |
| Fractional shares | Varies by broker | Usually available |

## Why Expense Ratios Matter

The expense ratio is the annual fee charged by the fund, taken from your returns.

```
$100,000 invested for 30 years at 10% return:

0.03% expense ratio → $1,719,884
0.50% expense ratio → $1,524,471
1.00% expense ratio → $1,348,349

Difference between cheapest and most expensive: $371,535!
```

**Always choose low-cost funds.** A difference of 0.5% compounds into tens or hundreds of thousands over a lifetime.

## The "Three-Fund Portfolio"

A simple, diversified portfolio used by many investors:

1. **US stocks** — VTI or VOO (60-80%)
2. **International stocks** — VXUS (10-20%)
3. **Bonds** — BND (10-30%)

That's it. Three funds, globally diversified, rock-bottom costs.

## Key Takeaway

> ETFs and index funds let you own the whole market at very low cost. For most people, a simple portfolio of 2-3 low-cost index ETFs will outperform stock picking.
