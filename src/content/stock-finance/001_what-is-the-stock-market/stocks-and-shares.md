# Stocks & Shares

## What is a Stock?

A **stock** (also called a **share** or **equity**) represents a small piece of ownership in a company. When you buy a stock, you become a **shareholder** — you literally own a fraction of that business.

If a company has issued 1,000,000 shares and you own 1,000, you own **0.1%** of the company.

## Why Do Stocks Have Value?

Stocks have value because they represent a claim on:
- **Earnings** — the company's profits
- **Assets** — the company's property, cash, and equipment
- **Future growth** — what the company might earn in the future

## Common Stock vs Preferred Stock

| Feature | Common Stock | Preferred Stock |
|---------|-------------|----------------|
| Voting rights | Yes (1 share = 1 vote) | Usually no |
| Dividends | Variable, not guaranteed | Fixed, paid first |
| Price growth | Higher potential | Lower potential |
| Bankruptcy priority | Last in line | Before common stock |

Most retail investors buy **common stock**.

## How Do You Make Money?

There are two ways to profit from stocks:

### 1. Capital Gains
Buy low, sell high. If you buy a share at $50 and sell at $75, your capital gain is $25.

### 2. Dividends
Some companies pay a portion of their profits to shareholders regularly. For example, a company might pay $2 per share per year. This is **passive income** — you get paid just for holding the stock.

## Key Takeaway

> A stock is ownership in a business. You profit when the business grows (capital gains) or shares its profits with you (dividends).
