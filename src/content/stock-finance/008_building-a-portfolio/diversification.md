# Diversification

## Don't Put All Your Eggs in One Basket

**Diversification** means spreading your investments across different assets so that a loss in one doesn't devastate your entire portfolio.

If you put 100% of your money in a single stock and that company goes bankrupt, you lose everything. But if you own 50 stocks across different industries, one bankruptcy barely dents your portfolio.

## Types of Diversification

### 1. Across Asset Classes
Hold a mix of stocks, bonds, and cash. When stocks fall, bonds often rise (or at least hold steady), cushioning your portfolio.

### 2. Across Sectors
Don't just buy tech stocks. Spread across:
- Technology
- Healthcare
- Finance
- Consumer goods
- Energy
- Industrials

**Example**: In 2022, tech stocks dropped ~30% while energy stocks gained ~60%. A diversified investor felt far less pain than a tech-only investor.

### 3. Across Company Sizes
Mix large-cap, mid-cap, and small-cap stocks:
- **Large-cap**: Stable, slower growth (Apple, Microsoft)
- **Mid-cap**: Balance of growth and stability
- **Small-cap**: Higher growth potential, higher risk

### 4. Geographic Diversification
Don't invest only in your home country:
- **US stocks**: ~60% of world market cap
- **International developed**: Europe, Japan, Australia
- **Emerging markets**: China, India, Brazil — higher risk/reward

### 5. Across Time (Dollar Cost Averaging)
Invest regularly over time rather than all at once — this diversifies your entry points.

## Correlation: The Key Concept

**Correlation** measures how two assets move relative to each other:

| Correlation | Meaning | Example |
|------------|---------|---------|
| **+1.0** | Move exactly together | Two tech stocks |
| **0** | No relationship | Stocks vs. gold |
| **-1.0** | Move in opposite directions | Stocks vs. government bonds (sometimes) |

The best diversification comes from holding assets with **low or negative correlation**.

## How Much is Enough?

Research shows:
- **1 stock**: Very high risk
- **10 stocks**: Eliminates ~75% of company-specific risk
- **30+ stocks**: Eliminates nearly all company-specific risk
- **Index fund**: Maximum diversification in one purchase

You **cannot** diversify away **market risk** (the risk that the entire market drops). But you can eliminate company-specific and sector-specific risk.

## The Easy Way: Index Funds

A single S&P 500 index fund gives you instant diversification across 500 large US companies. Add an international fund and a bond fund, and you have a well-diversified portfolio with just 3 holdings.

> **Key takeaway**: Diversification is the closest thing to a "free lunch" in investing — it reduces risk without necessarily reducing expected returns.
