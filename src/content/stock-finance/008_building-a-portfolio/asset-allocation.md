# Asset Allocation

## What is Asset Allocation?

**Asset allocation** is how you divide your investment money among different asset classes — primarily stocks, bonds, and cash. It's the single most important decision you'll make as an investor.

Studies show that asset allocation accounts for **over 90%** of the variation in portfolio returns over time — far more than individual stock picks.

## The Three Main Asset Classes

| Asset Class | Risk | Return Potential | Role in Portfolio |
|------------|------|-----------------|-------------------|
| **Stocks** | High | High | Growth — build wealth over time |
| **Bonds** | Low-Medium | Low-Medium | Stability — reduce volatility, provide income |
| **Cash** | Very Low | Very Low | Safety — emergency fund, short-term needs |

## Common Allocation Models

### Aggressive (80/20)
- 80% stocks, 20% bonds
- Higher risk, higher potential returns
- Suitable for younger investors with long time horizons

### Moderate (60/40)
- 60% stocks, 40% bonds
- Classic balanced portfolio
- Good for mid-career investors

### Conservative (30/70)
- 30% stocks, 70% bonds
- Lower risk, lower returns
- Suitable for retirees or short time horizons

## The Age-Based Rule of Thumb

A popular guideline:

> **Bond percentage = Your age**

- Age 25 → 25% bonds, 75% stocks
- Age 40 → 40% bonds, 60% stocks
- Age 65 → 65% bonds, 35% stocks

This is a starting point, not a strict rule. Adjust based on your risk tolerance, goals, and financial situation.

## Rebalancing

Over time, your allocation **drifts** as different assets perform differently. If stocks surge, your 60/40 portfolio might become 75/25.

**Rebalancing** means selling some winners and buying some laggards to return to your target allocation.

### When to Rebalance
- **Calendar-based**: Every 6 or 12 months
- **Threshold-based**: When any asset class drifts more than 5% from target

### Why It Matters
- Forces you to **buy low, sell high** systematically
- Keeps your risk level consistent
- Removes emotion from the process

## Beyond Stocks and Bonds

Advanced portfolios may include:
- **Real estate** (REITs)
- **Commodities** (gold, oil)
- **International stocks** (emerging markets)
- **Alternative investments** (crypto, private equity)

> **Key takeaway**: Your asset allocation should match your **time horizon**, **risk tolerance**, and **financial goals**. Get this right, and the details matter less.
