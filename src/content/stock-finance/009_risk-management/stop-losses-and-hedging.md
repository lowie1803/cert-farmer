# Stop Losses & Hedging

## Protecting Your Downside

Good investing isn't just about picking winners — it's about **limiting losses**. Here are practical tools to protect your portfolio.

## Stop Loss Orders

A **stop loss** automatically sells your stock when it drops to a certain price, limiting your loss.

### How It Works
- You buy a stock at $100
- You set a stop loss at $85
- If the stock drops to $85, it triggers a market order to sell
- Your maximum loss is capped at ~15%

### Trailing Stop Loss
Instead of a fixed price, it follows the stock **up** and only triggers on a pullback:

- You set a 10% trailing stop
- Stock rises from $100 to $150 → stop moves up to $135
- Stock drops from $150 to $135 → triggers a sell
- You lock in a $35 gain instead of riding it back down

### Pros and Cons

| Pros | Cons |
|------|------|
| Limits losses automatically | May sell during temporary dips |
| Removes emotion from selling | Gaps can cause execution below your stop |
| Lets you set it and forget it | Can be triggered by normal volatility |

## Position Sizing

**Never bet the farm on a single trade.** Position sizing means deciding **how much** to invest in each holding.

### The 1-2% Rule
Many investors risk no more than 1-2% of their total portfolio on any single position.

- Portfolio: $50,000
- Max risk per trade: $500-$1,000
- If you set a 10% stop loss, your position size should be $5,000-$10,000

### Why It Matters
If you risk 2% per trade, you can be wrong 10 times in a row and still have 80% of your portfolio. If you risk 20% per trade, three losses wipe out half your money.

## Hedging Basics

**Hedging** means taking a position that offsets potential losses in another investment.

### Hedging with Bonds
- When stocks fall, government bonds often rise
- A 60/40 stock/bond portfolio is a basic hedge

### Hedging with Options (Advanced)
- **Protective put**: Buy a put option on a stock you own — it gives you the right to sell at a set price, limiting your downside
- Think of it like insurance — costs money but protects against catastrophic loss

### Hedging with Gold
- Gold is often called a "safe haven" — tends to hold value during market crises
- Not always correlated with stocks

## The Emergency Fund

Before investing, maintain **3-6 months** of living expenses in a savings account. This isn't an investment — it's protection so you never have to sell investments at a bad time to cover unexpected expenses.

## Key Risk Management Rules

1. **Never invest money you can't afford to lose**
2. **Diversify** — across stocks, sectors, geographies, and asset classes
3. **Use stop losses** for individual stock positions
4. **Size positions appropriately** — no single bet should be able to ruin you
5. **Keep an emergency fund** — so you're never a forced seller
6. **Have a plan before you buy** — know your exit strategy

> **Key takeaway**: The best investors aren't the ones who never lose — they're the ones who manage their losses so they can stay in the game long enough to win.
