# Your First Trade

## Step-by-Step Guide

Making your first trade can feel intimidating. Here's exactly what to do.

## Step 1: Fund Your Account

- Transfer money from your bank to your brokerage account
- ACH transfers are free but take 1-3 business days
- Wire transfers are faster but may have fees
- Most brokers let you trade immediately with "instant deposits" up to a certain amount

## Step 2: Decide What to Buy

For your first investment, keep it simple:

### Best First Investment: A Broad Index ETF

| ETF | What It Tracks | Expense Ratio |
|-----|---------------|---------------|
| **VOO** | S&P 500 (500 largest US companies) | 0.03% |
| **VTI** | Total US stock market (~4,000 stocks) | 0.03% |
| **VT** | Total world stock market | 0.07% |

Why an index ETF? Instant diversification, low fees, proven long-term returns. You can always add individual stocks later.

## Step 3: Research Before Buying

Even for index funds, check:
- **Current price** and recent performance
- **Expense ratio** (lower is better)
- **Trading volume** (higher means easier to buy/sell)

For individual stocks, also check:
- Financial statements and key ratios (P/E, revenue growth)
- Recent news and analyst opinions
- What the company actually does and its competitive position

## Step 4: Place Your Order

1. **Search** for the ticker symbol (e.g., VOO)
2. **Choose order type**:
   - **Market order**: Buy immediately at current price (simplest)
   - **Limit order**: Set maximum price you'll pay (more control)
3. **Enter quantity**: Number of shares or dollar amount
4. **Review**: Double-check everything
5. **Submit**: Confirm the order

### Fractional Shares
Many brokers now support **fractional shares** — you don't need $500+ to buy one share of an expensive stock. You can invest as little as $1.

## Step 5: After Your Purchase

- **Don't panic check** your portfolio every hour
- **Set up recurring investments** if using DCA strategy
- **Keep a simple spreadsheet** tracking what you bought, when, and why
- **Review quarterly**, not daily

## Common First-Trade Mistakes to Avoid

| Mistake | Better Approach |
|---------|----------------|
| Buying a "hot tip" stock | Start with an index fund |
| Investing all your savings at once | Keep an emergency fund first |
| Using a market order on a volatile stock | Use a limit order |
| Selling after a 5% drop | Have a plan and stick to it |
| Checking your portfolio hourly | Check monthly at most |

## What Happens After You Buy

- Your shares appear in your account
- You can see real-time price changes
- Dividends (if any) will be deposited automatically
- You only "realize" a gain or loss when you actually sell

> **Key takeaway**: Don't wait for the "perfect" time. The best time to start investing was yesterday. The second best time is today. Start small, start simple, and learn as you go.
