# Common Beginner Mistakes

## Learning from Others' Expensive Lessons

Every investor makes mistakes. Here are the most common ones so you can avoid them.

## 1. Emotional Trading

**The mistake**: Buying when excited (FOMO) and selling when scared (panic).

The market dropped 10%? Panic sell everything. A stock is up 50% this month? Buy at the top.

**The fix**: Have a written investment plan. Decide **before** you invest what would make you buy more and what would make you sell. Then follow the plan.

## 2. Trying to Time the Market

**The mistake**: Waiting for the "perfect" entry point or trying to sell before a crash.

**The reality**: Missing just the 10 best trading days over a 20-year period cuts your returns nearly in half. Nobody can consistently predict those days.

| Scenario (S&P 500, 2003-2022) | Annualized Return |
|------|---------|
| Stayed invested the whole time | ~9.8% |
| Missed the 10 best days | ~5.6% |
| Missed the 20 best days | ~2.9% |
| Missed the 30 best days | ~0.8% |

**The fix**: Time **in** the market beats tim**ing** the market. Invest regularly and stay invested.

## 3. Not Diversifying

**The mistake**: Putting all your money into one stock, one sector, or one country.

**The fix**: Use index funds for broad diversification. If you pick individual stocks, own at least 15-20 across different sectors.

## 4. Ignoring Fees

**The mistake**: Paying 1-2% in fund fees because "it's just 1%."

Over 30 years, a 1% fee difference can cost you **tens of thousands of dollars**. A $10,000 investment at 10% return:
- 0.05% fee → $171,354
- 1.00% fee → $142,429
- Difference: **$28,925** lost to fees

**The fix**: Choose low-cost index funds (expense ratio under 0.20%).

## 5. FOMO Buying (Fear of Missing Out)

**The mistake**: Buying a stock because it's all over social media, everyone's talking about it, and it's up 200%.

By the time something is obvious to everyone, the easy money has been made. Late buyers often become bag holders.

**The fix**: If you didn't do the research yourself, don't buy it. "Hot tips" are almost always too late.

## 6. Panic Selling

**The mistake**: Selling everything after a market drop.

Market corrections (10%+ drops) happen **about once a year**. Bear markets (20%+ drops) happen every 3-5 years. If you sell every time, you lock in losses and miss the recovery.

**The fix**: Zoom out. The S&P 500 has recovered from every crash in history. If your investments are diversified and your time horizon is long, dips are temporary.

## 7. No Investment Plan

**The mistake**: Buying random stocks with no strategy, goal, or timeline.

**The fix**: Answer these questions before investing:
- What am I investing for? (Retirement, house, education)
- When will I need the money? (5 years? 20 years?)
- How much risk can I handle emotionally?
- What's my asset allocation? (Stocks/bonds/cash split)

## 8. Checking Your Portfolio Too Often

**The mistake**: Looking at your portfolio every day (or every hour). The market drops 2% and you stress about it.

On any given day, the market is roughly a coin flip. Over 20+ years, it's gone up ~95% of the time.

**The fix**: Check monthly or quarterly. Automate your investments and stop watching.

## 9. Investing Money You'll Need Soon

**The mistake**: Investing rent money or emergency fund in stocks.

Stocks can drop 30%+ in a year. If you need that money soon, you might be forced to sell at a loss.

**The fix**: Keep 3-6 months of expenses in a savings account. Only invest money you won't need for 5+ years.

> **Key takeaway**: Most investing mistakes are emotional, not analytical. The best investors are patient, disciplined, and boring. Exciting investing is usually bad investing.
