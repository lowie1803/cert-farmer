# Passive vs Active Investing

## Two Approaches to Managing Your Money

The debate between passive and active investing is one of the most important in personal finance. Here's what each means and the data behind them.

## Passive Investing

**Buy and hold a broad market index fund. Don't try to beat the market — just match it.**

### How It Works
- Buy an index fund that tracks a market benchmark (S&P 500, total stock market)
- Hold it for years or decades
- Rebalance occasionally
- Ignore daily market noise

### Key Characteristics
| Feature | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| Goal | Match market returns |
| Fees | Very low (0.03–0.20% per year) |
| Time required | Minimal — set and forget |
| Trading frequency | Very low |
| Tax efficiency | High (fewer taxable events) |

### The Case for Passive
- **Over 90%** of actively managed funds underperform their benchmark over 15 years
- Lower fees compound into massive savings over decades
- S&P 500 has returned ~10% annually over the long term
- Warren Buffett himself recommends index funds for most investors

## Active Investing

**Try to beat the market by picking stocks, timing trades, and making strategic bets.**

### How It Works
- Research individual companies
- Buy stocks you believe will outperform
- Sell when you think they're overvalued
- Adjust positions based on market conditions

### Key Characteristics
| Feature | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| Goal | Beat market returns |
| Fees | Higher (research tools, commissions, fund fees 0.5–2%) |
| Time required | Significant — ongoing research |
| Trading frequency | High |
| Tax efficiency | Lower (more capital gains events) |

### The Case for Active
- Potential for higher returns (if you're skilled or lucky)
- Can avoid or reduce exposure during downturns
- Some investors genuinely enjoy the process
- Certain market inefficiencies can be exploited

## The Data: Who Wins?

### SPIVA Scorecard (S&P vs Active)
Over a 15-year period:
- **92%** of large-cap fund managers underperformed the S&P 500
- **95%** of mid-cap fund managers underperformed their benchmark
- **97%** of small-cap fund managers underperformed their benchmark

### The Fee Problem
Even a small fee difference compounds dramatically:

| | Passive Fund (0.05%) | Active Fund (1.0%) |
|---|---|---|
| $10,000 invested for 30 years at 10% | **$171,354** | **$142,429** |
| **Lost to fees** | $1,447 | **$30,372** |

That's nearly **$29,000** more in fees for the active fund — with worse expected performance.

## The Best of Both Worlds: Core-Satellite

Many investors combine both approaches:
- **Core (80-90%)**: Low-cost index funds — your foundation
- **Satellite (10-20%)**: Individual stocks or sector bets — for fun and potential alpha

This gives you reliable market returns while scratching the itch to pick stocks, without risking your financial future.

> **Key takeaway**: For most investors, passive investing is the clear winner. The data overwhelmingly shows that trying to beat the market costs more and delivers less over the long run.
