# Tickers & Price Quotes

## What is a Ticker Symbol?

A **ticker** (or stock symbol) is a short abbreviation used to identify a publicly traded company:

| Ticker | Company |
|--------|---------|
| AAPL | Apple |
| MSFT | Microsoft |
| GOOGL | Alphabet (Google) |
| TSLA | Tesla |
| AMZN | Amazon |
| BRK.B | Berkshire Hathaway (Class B) |

- NYSE tickers are usually 1-3 letters (e.g., F, GE, JPM)
- NASDAQ tickers are usually 4 letters (e.g., AAPL, MSFT)
- ETFs also have tickers (e.g., SPY, QQQ, VOO)

## Reading a Stock Quote

When you look up a stock, you'll see something like this:

```
AAPL - Apple Inc.
$175.50  +2.30 (+1.33%)

Open:    $173.80    High:     $176.20
Low:     $173.50    Close:    $175.50
Volume:  58.2M      Avg Vol:  62.1M
Mkt Cap: $2.72T     P/E:      29.4
52w Hi:  $199.62    52w Lo:   $143.90
Div:     $0.96      Yield:    0.55%
```

## Key Fields Explained

| Field | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| **Price** | Current (or last) trading price |
| **Change (+/-)** | Price change from previous close, in dollars and percent |
| **Open** | Price at market open (9:30 AM) |
| **High / Low** | Highest and lowest price of the day |
| **Volume** | Number of shares traded today |
| **Avg Volume** | Average daily volume (typically 30-day) |
| **Market Cap** | Total value of all shares (price x shares outstanding) |
| **P/E Ratio** | Price-to-Earnings ratio (valuation metric) |
| **52-week High/Low** | Highest and lowest price in the past year |
| **Dividend / Yield** | Annual dividend per share / dividend as % of price |

## Bid and Ask

- **Bid**: The highest price a buyer is willing to pay
- **Ask**: The lowest price a seller is willing to accept
- **Spread**: Ask minus Bid — a smaller spread means more liquidity

```
Bid: $175.48 x 300    Ask: $175.52 x 500
Spread: $0.04
```

The "x 300" means there are 300 shares available at that price.

## Where to Look Up Quotes

Free tools: Google Finance, Yahoo Finance, TradingView, your broker's app.

## Key Takeaway

> A stock quote gives you a snapshot of a stock's current state. Focus on price, volume, and the bid-ask spread to understand trading conditions.
