# Candlestick Charts

## Why Charts Matter

Price charts are the primary visual tool for understanding stock behavior. They show you at a glance what happened over time — trends, volatility, and momentum.

## Anatomy of a Candlestick

Each candlestick represents a **time period** (1 day, 1 hour, 1 week, etc.) and shows four prices:

```
    │          ← Upper wick (shadow)
    │
  ┌───┐       ← High of the body
  │   │
  │   │       ← Body (open to close)
  │   │
  └───┘       ← Low of the body
    │
    │          ← Lower wick (shadow)
```

- **Open**: Price at the start of the period
- **Close**: Price at the end of the period
- **High**: Highest price during the period
- **Low**: Lowest price during the period

## Green vs Red

- **Green (bullish)**: Close > Open — price went UP
- **Red (bearish)**: Close < Open — price went DOWN

The **body** shows the range between open and close. The **wicks** show the full high-low range.

## Common Candlestick Patterns

### Single Candle Patterns

| Pattern | Shape | Signal |
|---------|-------|--------|
| **Doji** | Tiny body, long wicks | Indecision — potential reversal |
| **Hammer** | Small body at top, long lower wick | Bullish reversal (at bottom of downtrend) |
| **Shooting Star** | Small body at bottom, long upper wick | Bearish reversal (at top of uptrend) |
| **Marubozu** | Full body, no wicks | Strong momentum in that direction |

### Multi-Candle Patterns

| Pattern | Description | Signal |
|---------|-------------|--------|
| **Engulfing** | Second candle's body completely covers the first | Reversal |
| **Morning Star** | Down candle → small candle → up candle | Bullish reversal |
| **Evening Star** | Up candle → small candle → down candle | Bearish reversal |

## Chart Timeframes

| Timeframe | Best For |
|-----------|----------|
| 1-min, 5-min | Day trading |
| 1-hour, 4-hour | Swing trading |
| Daily | Most investors (default) |
| Weekly, monthly | Long-term trends |

## Key Takeaway

> Candlestick charts pack four data points into each candle. Green means up, red means down. Start with daily charts and learn to spot basic patterns like doji, hammer, and engulfing.
