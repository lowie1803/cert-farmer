# Volume & Market Cap

## Volume

**Volume** is the number of shares traded during a specific time period.

### Why Volume Matters

- **Confirms trends**: A price move on high volume is more significant than one on low volume
- **Signals interest**: Surging volume often precedes big price moves
- **Measures liquidity**: High-volume stocks are easier to buy and sell at fair prices

### Reading Volume

```
Daily volume:    58,000,000 shares
Average volume:  45,000,000 shares (30-day average)
```

Volume **above average** = unusual interest (earnings, news, breakout).
Volume **below average** = quiet day, less conviction behind price moves.

### Volume Rules of Thumb

| Scenario | Interpretation |
|----------|---------------|
| Price up + high volume | Strong bullish signal |
| Price up + low volume | Weak rally, may not last |
| Price down + high volume | Strong selling pressure |
| Price down + low volume | Light profit-taking, not panic |

## Market Capitalization

**Market cap** = Share price x Total shares outstanding

It measures the **total value** the market assigns to a company.

### Size Categories

| Category | Market Cap | Examples |
|----------|-----------|----------|
| **Mega-cap** | > $200B | Apple, Microsoft, Amazon |
| **Large-cap** | $10B – $200B | Starbucks, FedEx, AMD |
| **Mid-cap** | $2B – $10B | Etsy, Crocs, Zillow |
| **Small-cap** | $300M – $2B | Many regional companies |
| **Micro-cap** | < $300M | Very small, higher risk |

### Why Market Cap Matters

- **Risk indicator**: Larger companies are generally more stable
- **Portfolio building**: Diversify across cap sizes
- **Index eligibility**: The S&P 500 requires ~$14.5B+ market cap
- **Comparison tool**: Better than share price for comparing companies

### Share Price is NOT Company Size

A common beginner mistake:

```
Company A: $500/share, 10M shares → Market cap: $5B (mid-cap)
Company B: $20/share, 5B shares  → Market cap: $100B (large-cap)
```

Company B is 20x larger despite a much lower share price.

## Key Takeaway

> Volume tells you how actively a stock is being traded — high volume confirms moves. Market cap tells you the company's total size — always compare by market cap, never just share price.
