# Moving Averages

## What is a Moving Average?

A **moving average (MA)** smooths out price data by calculating the average price over a specific number of periods. It helps you see the **trend** without the noise of daily fluctuations.

## SMA vs EMA

### Simple Moving Average (SMA)
- Calculates the **arithmetic mean** of the last N closing prices
- Example: 10-day SMA = sum of last 10 closes ÷ 10
- Treats all days equally
- Slower to react to price changes

### Exponential Moving Average (EMA)
- Gives **more weight to recent prices**
- Reacts faster to new price movements
- Better for short-term trading
- Formula applies a multiplier that emphasizes recent data

| Feature | SMA | EMA |
|---------|-----|-----|
| Speed | Slower | Faster |
| Sensitivity | Less sensitive | More sensitive |
| Best for | Long-term trends | Short-term signals |
| Noise | Less noise | More responsive but noisier |

## Common Periods

| Period | Use Case |
|--------|----------|
| **10-day** | Short-term momentum |
| **20-day** | Short-term trend |
| **50-day** | Medium-term trend — widely watched |
| **200-day** | Long-term trend — the "big picture" line |

The **50-day** and **200-day** moving averages are the most important for most investors.

## Golden Cross & Death Cross

These are powerful signals formed by two moving averages crossing:

### Golden Cross (Bullish)
- The **50-day MA crosses above** the 200-day MA
- Signals the start of a potential long-term uptrend
- Historically associated with strong bull markets

### Death Cross (Bearish)
- The **50-day MA crosses below** the 200-day MA
- Signals a potential long-term downtrend
- Often triggers selling pressure

## Using MAs for Buy/Sell Signals

### Price + MA
- **Buy signal**: Price crosses **above** a key MA (e.g., 50-day)
- **Sell signal**: Price crosses **below** a key MA

### MA + MA (Crossover Strategy)
- **Buy**: Short-term MA crosses above long-term MA
- **Sell**: Short-term MA crosses below long-term MA

## Limitations

- Moving averages are **lagging indicators** — they follow price, not predict it
- They work best in **trending markets** and give false signals in sideways markets
- No single MA period is "best" — it depends on your timeframe and strategy

> **Tip**: Don't rely on moving averages alone. Combine them with volume, support/resistance, and other indicators for stronger signals.
