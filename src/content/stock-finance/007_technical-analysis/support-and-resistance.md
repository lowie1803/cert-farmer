# Support & Resistance

## What Are They?

**Support** and **resistance** are price levels where buying or selling pressure tends to be strong enough to prevent the price from moving further.

- **Support**: A price level where demand is strong enough to stop the price from falling further — like a "floor"
- **Resistance**: A price level where selling pressure is strong enough to stop the price from rising further — like a "ceiling"

## How to Identify Support & Resistance

### Historical Price Levels
Look for prices where the stock has **repeatedly bounced** or **stalled**:
- If a stock bounces off $50 three times, $50 is strong support
- If it fails to break above $100 multiple times, $100 is strong resistance

### Psychological Price Levels
Round numbers act as natural support/resistance:
- $10, $50, $100, $500, $1,000
- Traders tend to place orders at round numbers

### Moving Averages
The 50-day and 200-day moving averages often act as dynamic support/resistance levels.

### Volume
High volume at a specific price level suggests many traders have positions there, making it a stronger support/resistance zone.

## Role Reversal

One of the most important concepts:
- When **support is broken**, it often becomes **resistance**
- When **resistance is broken**, it often becomes **support**

Example: A stock breaks below $50 support. If it tries to recover, $50 now acts as resistance.

## Breakouts and Fakeouts

### Breakouts
When price moves **through** a support or resistance level with strong volume:
- Breaking above resistance = bullish signal
- Breaking below support = bearish signal

### Fakeouts
Sometimes price briefly crosses a level but quickly reverses — a **false breakout**:
- Wait for **confirmation** (e.g., a close above resistance, not just an intraday touch)
- Look for volume: real breakouts usually have **higher than average volume**

## Practical Tips

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| Think of support/resistance as **zones**, not exact lines | Expect price to stop at an exact dollar amount |
| Use multiple timeframes to confirm levels | Rely on a single chart |
| Wait for confirmation before trading a breakout | Chase every breakout immediately |
| Combine with other indicators (volume, RSI) | Use support/resistance alone |
