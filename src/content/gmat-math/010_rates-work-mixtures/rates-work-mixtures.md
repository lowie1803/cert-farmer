# Rates, Work & Mixtures

## Distance-Rate-Time (D = R × T)

The fundamental formula: **Distance = Rate × Time**

Rearrangements:
- R = D/T
- T = D/R

> A car travels 240 miles at 60 mph. How long?
T = 240/60 = **4 hours**

### Average Speed

**Average speed ≠ average of speeds** when distances are unequal.

Average speed = Total Distance / Total Time

> Car goes A→B at 40 mph and B→A at 60 mph. Average speed?

Assume distance = 120 miles each way:
- Time A→B = 120/40 = 3 hr
- Time B→A = 120/60 = 2 hr
- Total: 240 mi / 5 hr = **48 mph**

Formula for equal distances: **2ab/(a+b)** (harmonic mean)

### Relative Speed

**Moving toward each other**: add speeds.  
**Moving in same direction**: subtract speeds.

> Two trains 300 miles apart approach each other at 60 and 90 mph. Time to meet?

Combined speed = 150 mph. Time = 300/150 = **2 hours**

## Work Problems

If someone completes a job in n hours, their rate is **1/n job per hour**.

**Combined work**: add the rates, then find total time.

> A finishes in 6 hrs, B in 4 hrs. Together?

Rate_A + Rate_B = 1/6 + 1/4 = 2/12 + 3/12 = 5/12 per hour

Time together = 12/5 = **2.4 hours**

### Partial Work Setup

> A works for 5 days, then B joins. Total job finishes when?

If A does 1/10 per day:
- After 5 days: A has done 1/2 the job.
- Remaining: 1/2.
- Together (rate 1/6 per day): time = (1/2)/(1/6) = **3 more days**

## Mixtures & Weighted Averages

**Mixture formula**: C₁V₁ + C₂V₂ = C_final × V_final

where C = concentration, V = volume.

> Mix 10 lbs at $4/lb with x lbs at $6/lb. Desired average: $5/lb.

10×4 + x×6 = 5×(10+x)
40 + 6x = 50 + 5x
x = **10 lbs**

### Alligation (quick visual method)

```
$4        $6
   \    /
    $5
   /    \
 6-5=1  5-4=1
```
Ratio = 1:1, so 10 lbs each.

### Adding Pure Substance

> A 30-liter solution is 20% acid. How much pure acid to add to make it 40%?

Current acid: 0.20 × 30 = 6 liters
Let x = liters of pure acid added:
6 + x = 0.40(30 + x)
6 + x = 12 + 0.4x
0.6x = 6
x = **10 liters**

## Worked Examples

### Example 1: Downstream/upstream
Boat speed in still water = 12 mph. Current = 3 mph.
- Downstream speed = 15 mph → time for 45 miles = 45/15 = **3 hours**
- Upstream speed = 9 mph → time for 45 miles = 45/9 = 5 hours

### Example 2: Work with different rates
Pipe A fills a tank in 6 hours, Pipe B drains it in 12 hours. Both open, starting empty — how long to fill?
Net rate = 1/6 − 1/12 = 2/12 − 1/12 = 1/12 per hour → **12 hours**

## Common Traps

- **Average speed**: Never average the speeds; use total distance / total time.
- **Work rates are additive**: If A and B together complete 5/12 per hour, the time is 12/5 — not (6+4)/2 = 5.
- **Mixture bases change**: When you add liquid, the total volume increases.
- **Negative work (draining)**: A draining pipe subtracts from the fill rate.
