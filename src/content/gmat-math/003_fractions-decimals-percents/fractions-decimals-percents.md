# Fractions, Decimals & Percents

## Fractions

A fraction a/b means a ÷ b, where b ≠ 0.

### Operations
- **Addition/Subtraction**: Find LCD, then add/subtract numerators.
  - 1/4 + 1/6 = 3/12 + 2/12 = 5/12
- **Multiplication**: Multiply numerators × numerators, denominators × denominators.
  - 2/3 × 3/5 = 6/15 = 2/5
- **Division**: Multiply by the reciprocal.
  - 2/3 ÷ 4/9 = 2/3 × 9/4 = 18/12 = 3/2

### Simplifying (Reducing)
Divide numerator and denominator by their GCD.
- 18/24 → GCD(18,24)=6 → 3/4

## Decimals

### Converting Fractions to Decimals
Divide numerator by denominator:
- 3/4 = 0.75
- 1/3 = 0.333... (repeating)

### Common Conversions to Memorize

| Fraction | Decimal | Percent |
|----------|---------|---------|
| 1/2 | 0.5 | 50% |
| 1/3 | 0.333... | 33.3% |
| 2/3 | 0.667... | 66.7% |
| 1/4 | 0.25 | 25% |
| 3/4 | 0.75 | 75% |
| 1/5 | 0.2 | 20% |
| 1/8 | 0.125 | 12.5% |
| 5/8 | 0.625 | 62.5% |

## Percents

Percent means "per hundred": x% = x/100 = x × 0.01

### Three Core Percent Problems

1. **Find the percent of a number**: What is 30% of 80?
   - 80 × 0.30 = 24

2. **Find what percent one number is of another**: 18 is what percent of 72?
   - 18/72 = 0.25 = 25%

3. **Reverse percent (find the original)**: 15 is 30% of what?
   - 15 / 0.30 = 50

### Percent Change

$$\text{Percent Change} = \frac{\text{New} - \text{Old}}{\text{Old}} \times 100\%$$

- Increase from $80 to $100: (100−80)/80 × 100 = 25% increase
- Decrease from $100 to $80: (80−100)/100 × 100 = −20% decrease

> The base always matters. A 20% increase followed by a 20% decrease does NOT return to the original.
> 1.20 × 0.80 = 0.96 → **4% net decrease**

### Successive Percent Changes

Multiply the multipliers:
- 30% off, then 10% off: 0.70 × 0.90 = 0.63 → 37% total discount (not 40%)

### Reverse Discount Problems

> A jacket costs $120 **after** a 20% discount. What was the original price?

- If 20% was taken off, you paid 80% of the original.
- Original × 0.80 = 120 → Original = 120 / 0.80 = **$150**

Common mistake: adding 20% back ($120 × 1.20 = $144) — this is wrong.

## Worked Examples

### Example 1: Percent of a percent
A price increases 50% and then decreases 50%. Net change?
1.50 × 0.50 = 0.75 → **25% net decrease**

### Example 2: Comparing fractions
Is 7/11 or 5/8 larger?
Cross-multiply: 7×8=56 vs. 5×11=55. 56>55, so 7/11 > 5/8.

### Example 3: Converting repeating decimal
0.333... = 1/3. 0.666... = 2/3. 0.125 = 1/8 (exact, not repeating).

## Common Traps

- **Percent vs. percentage points**: A rate going from 10% to 15% is a 5 percentage-point increase, but a 50% increase in rate.
- **Percent of vs. percent more than**: 60% of 80 = 48; 60% more than 80 = 80 × 1.60 = 128.
- **The base changes**: "A is 20% more than B" → A = 1.2B. "B is 20% less than A" → B = 0.8A. These are different statements.
