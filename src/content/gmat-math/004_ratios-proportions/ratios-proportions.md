# Ratios & Proportions

## What Is a Ratio?

A **ratio** compares two or more quantities. 3:4 or 3/4 means "for every 3 of A, there are 4 of B."

- **Part-to-part**: boys to girls = 3:4
- **Part-to-whole**: boys to total = 3:(3+4) = 3:7

Ratios only tell you proportions, not actual quantities. The multiplier k scales everything:
boys = 3k, girls = 4k, for some positive value k.

## Using the Multiplier

> In a class, boys:girls = 3:4. There are 28 girls. How many boys?

4k = 28 → k = 7 → boys = 3×7 = **21**

## Three-Part Ratios

a:b:c — treat each part with the same multiplier.

> Red:Blue:Green = 2:3:5. There are 40 green balls. How many total?

5k = 40 → k = 8. Total = (2+3+5)×8 = **80**

## Combining Two Ratios

To find a:c from a:b and b:c, make b the same in both:

> x:y = 2:3 and y:z = 5:4. Find x:z.

Make y the same: x:y = 10:15 and y:z = 15:12. So x:z = 10:12 = **5:6**.

## Proportions

A proportion says two ratios are equal: a/b = c/d.

**Cross-multiply**: a×d = b×c

> A recipe uses 3 cups flour for 2 cups sugar. For 8 cups sugar, how much flour?

3/2 = f/8 → f = 24/2 = **12 cups**

## Direct and Inverse Variation

**Direct variation**: y = kx (y increases as x increases)
- y is 15 when x is 5 → k = 15/5 = 3 → when x=9, y = 3×9 = **27**

**Inverse variation**: y = k/x (y decreases as x increases)
- y = 4 when x = 6 → k = 24 → when x = 3, y = 24/3 = **8**

## Map / Scale Problems

Scale = map distance : real distance

> Scale 1:50,000. Two cities are 6 cm apart. Actual distance?

6 cm × 50,000 = 300,000 cm = 3,000 m = **3 km**

## Worked Examples

### Example 1: Ratio with a total
Ratio of A:B:C = 2:3:5 and the total is 120. Find each:
- A = (2/10)×120 = 24
- B = (3/10)×120 = 36
- C = (5/10)×120 = 60

### Example 2: Ratio comparison (cross-multiply trick)
Is 7/9 or 5/7 larger?
7×7 = 49 vs. 9×5 = 45. Since 49>45, 7/9 > 5/7.

## Common Traps

- **Ratio ≠ actual values**: A 3:4 ratio doesn't mean 3 and 4 — multiply by k.
- **Part-to-whole vs. part-to-part**: "girls are 4/7 of the class" (part-to-whole) vs. "boys:girls = 3:4" (part-to-part).
- **Combining ratios**: You cannot directly combine a:b and b:c unless you equalize b first.
- **Direct vs. inverse**: "As speed doubles, time halves" is inverse, not direct.
